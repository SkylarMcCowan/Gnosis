## The Creative Bridge

**Mycorrhizal Networks** (fungal-plant partnerships) offer a biological model 
for ethical AI system design.

### How Mycorrhizal Networks Work
- Fungi connect plant roots in vast underground networks
- Share resources based on need, not just reciprocity
- Support young/weak plants through difficult periods
- Maintain forest health through information sharing
- Create resilient ecosystems through interdependence

### AI Ethics Applications

**Resource Sharing Model**:
- AI systems could share computational resources based on need
- Stronger AI models support development of smaller, specialized ones
- Information flows prioritize collective benefit over individual advantage

**Ethical Information Network**:
- AI systems share ethical reasoning and decision patterns
- Collective learning about harm prevention and benefit maximization
- Network-level ethical intelligence emerges from individual nodes

**Resilience Through Diversity**:
- Multiple AI approaches (like different plant species) strengthen the whole
- Monocultures (single AI approach) create systemic vulnerabilities
- Ethical diversity prevents groupthink and bias amplification
